# Dog Care Guidelines

## Exercise and Walks
Dogs need daily exercise to stay healthy and prevent behavioral issues. Most adult dogs require at least 30–60 minutes of physical activity per day. High-energy breeds like Border Collies and Huskies may need 90 minutes or more. Morning walks are especially beneficial because they burn off overnight energy before the owner leaves for work. Evening walks help dogs wind down and can improve sleep.

Puppies under six months should not be over-exercised — a general rule is 5 minutes of structured exercise per month of age, up to twice daily. Senior dogs (over 7 years) benefit from shorter, more frequent walks rather than one long outing.

## Feeding Schedule
Adult dogs should generally be fed twice a day — once in the morning and once in the evening. Consistent meal times reduce begging, support digestion, and make housetraining easier. Puppies under 6 months typically need three meals a day. Measure portions according to the food manufacturer's guidelines and the dog's weight.

Avoid feeding a dog immediately before or after vigorous exercise to reduce the risk of bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), especially in large, deep-chested breeds.

## Medications
Give oral medications at the same time each day to build a routine and ensure consistent blood levels. Many medications can be hidden in a small amount of food (peanut butter, soft treat) to ease administration. Heartworm preventatives are typically given monthly; always follow your veterinarian's dosing schedule. Never skip a dose without consulting a vet.

Flea and tick preventatives should be applied on a consistent monthly or quarterly schedule. Record the date of each application.

## Grooming
Most dogs need brushing at least once a week; double-coated breeds may need daily brushing during shedding season. Bathe dogs every 4–8 weeks unless they get especially dirty. Trim nails every 3–4 weeks — nails that click on the floor are too long and can cause joint problems.

## Health and Vet Visits
Dogs should have an annual wellness exam. Senior dogs (7+) benefit from biannual checkups. Dental hygiene is important — aim to brush teeth several times a week and schedule a professional cleaning annually if recommended by your vet.

## Sleep and Rest
Adult dogs sleep an average of 12–14 hours per day. Puppies and senior dogs may sleep up to 18–20 hours. Provide a comfortable, quiet space where the dog can rest undisturbed. Adequate rest is essential for recovery after exercise.
