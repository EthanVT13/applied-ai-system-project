# Cat Care Guidelines

## Feeding
Most adult cats do well with two measured meals per day. Free-feeding (leaving food out all day) can lead to obesity, especially in indoor cats. Wet food provides additional hydration and is beneficial for cats prone to urinary issues. Cats are obligate carnivores — their diet must be high in protein.

Kittens under 6 months need three to four small meals daily. Senior cats (11+) may benefit from smaller, more frequent meals and foods formulated for older cats. Always ensure fresh water is available; many cats prefer running water (a pet fountain can help).

## Playtime and Enrichment
Cats need mental and physical stimulation to prevent boredom and obesity. Aim for at least two 10–15 minute interactive play sessions per day. Wand toys, feather teasers, and laser pointers (always end with a physical toy the cat can "catch") are effective. Schedule playtime before meals to mimic a natural hunt-eat-groom-sleep cycle.

Indoor cats especially need enrichment — window perches, puzzle feeders, and vertical climbing spaces (cat trees) support their natural behaviors.

## Medications and Eye Care
Administer eye drops or ear medications in a calm, consistent environment — ideally the same time each day. Gently restrain the cat, tilt the head back, and apply drops without touching the dropper tip to the eye. Offer a treat immediately after to create a positive association. For oral medications, pill pockets or compounded liquid formulations can make dosing easier.

## Litter Box
Provide one litter box per cat, plus one extra. Scoop at least once daily; deep-clean the box weekly. Cats are fastidious — a dirty litter box is the leading cause of inappropriate elimination.

## Grooming
Short-haired cats groom themselves effectively but benefit from weekly brushing to reduce hairballs. Long-haired cats may need daily brushing. Trim nails every 2–3 weeks. Dental care (brushing or dental treats) should be part of a regular routine.

## Vet Visits
Annual wellness exams are recommended for adult cats. Senior cats (11+) should see a vet every 6 months. Keep vaccinations current per your veterinarian's schedule.

## Sleep
Cats sleep 12–16 hours per day on average. They are crepuscular — most active at dawn and dusk. Respect quiet rest periods and avoid scheduling high-energy playtime right before your cat's usual nap windows.
